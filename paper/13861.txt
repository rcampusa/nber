Enrique G. Mendoza and Vivian Z. Yue

March 2008, Revised April 2010
JEL No. E32,E44,F32,F34

                                             ABSTRACT

We propose a model that solves the crucial disconnect between business cycle models that treat default
risk as an exogenous interest rate on working capital, and sovereign default models that treat output
fluctuations as an exogenous process with ad-hoc default costs. The model explains observed output
dynamics around defaults, countercyclical spreads, high debt ratios, and key business cycle moments.
Three features of the model are central for these results: working capital loans pay for imported inputs;
default triggers an efficiency loss as imported inputs are replaced by imperfect domestic substitutes;
and default on public and private foreign obligations occurs simultaneously.
