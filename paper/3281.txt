         CAPITAL MARKET INTEGRATION:   ISSUES OF INTERNATIONAL TAXATION


                                   ABSTRACT


     The paper analyzes three issues in international taxation: (a) How the
opening of the economy to international capital movements affects the size and
structure of the fiscal branch of government: (b) Optimal restrictions on
capital exports in the face of capital flight; and (c) The structure of taxes
on mobile factors which emerges from international tax competitions and the
gains from international tax harmonization.
