John Y. Campbell and Glen B. Taksler

May 2002
JEL No. G12



                                              ABSTRACT

        This paper explores the effect of equity volatility on corporate bond yields. Panel data for the late
1990's show that idiosyncratic firm-level volatility can explain as much cross-sectional variation in yields
as can credit ratings. This finding, together with the upward trend in idiosyncratic equity volatility
documented by Campbell, Lettau, Malkiel, and Xu (2001), helps to explain recent increases in corporate
bond yields.
