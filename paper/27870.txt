                                         ABSTRACT

There is growing interest in impact investing, the idea of deploying capital to obtain both
financial and social or environmental returns. Examination of every equity investment made by
the International Finance Corporation, one of the largest and longest-operating impact investors,
across 130 emerging market and developing economies shows that this portfolio has
outperformed the S&P 500 by 15 percent. Investments in larger economies have higher returns,
and returns decline as banking systems deepen and countries relax capital controls. These results
are consistent with imperfect integration of international capital markets, and a core thesis of
impact investing that some eligible markets do not receive sufficient investment capital.
